SingStyleTransfer: Genre-Based Singing Style Transfer

Voice style transfer has focused on transforming speech between speakers, leaving singing style, independent of speaker, unexplored. We investigated a VAE-GAN to perform singing style transfer across genres. This model is evaluated on the SingStyle111 dataset for its ability to carry out genre-to-genre transformations, opening new possibilities for music production and vocal synthesis.

MelGenFuse: Personalized Music Genre Fusion

Music composition has witnessed significant advancements with the infusion of artificial intelligence, particularly using Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) networks. This study introduces a novel, two-phase algorithm for personalized fusion music generation that reflects the composer's individual preferences. In the first phase, melodies are generated for individual genres using Recurrent Neural Networks (RNNs) employing techniques like Sequential, Dense, and one-hot encoding. These generated melodies serve as input for the second phase, where an LSTM network fuses them into a coherent composition. Notably, the algorithm incorporates weights set by the composer for each genre, allowing for a personalized composition. A stochastic approach is employed in both phases to introduce creative variance while balancing structural coherence.

LSTM RNN Genre Fusion

The OceanMaker

I scored the animated short film The OceanMaker for the 2024 Marvin Hamlisch Film Scoring Competition. To accompany this short animated action film, I developed an epic orchestral score. I drew inspiration from the action tracks of Hans Zimmer and themes composed by Michael Giacchino, incorporating emphatic and busy percussion lines and repeating piano motifs throughout the film.

SHIFT

Rescore for the short indie film SHIFT for the 2024 Indie Film Music Competition. This 3-minute film provides a glimpse into the life of a nurse living in her car to isolate in order to care for patients during the Covid-19 pandemic. She is kept from her family as she attempts to mask the pain of isolation from her son. Accompanying this film, I developed a melancholic score that balances both comfort and loneliness by using a childish and simple piano theme inspired by Michael Giacchino's score from Up.
